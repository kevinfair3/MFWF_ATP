\chapter{Conclusion and Next Steps}
\sffamily

In summary, the ATPW team within the MFWF project has completed several of the key objectives set out at the beginning of the semester. The aerodynamic test platform has a completed inner frame for mounting of the MFWF in such a way that transmits only the orthogonal forces to the load cells. Rigorous testing has been performed throughout the semester to verify the functionality of the aerodynamic test platform. While major design changes were needed in terms of the electronics, the platform is now capable of providing accurate force readings down to 30 grams of applied static load. In the event of a required redesign of the platform, preliminary designs have been developed in the attempts to rasing the resolution of the measured forces.

{\color{blue}
\underline{Conclusion of two semesters}

The ATPW neural-network team also established a strong two-semester foundation for machine-learning-based aerodynamic prediction. In the Fall 2025 semester, the team used a large NACA airfoil dataset as a proof-of-concept problem to compare candidate model architectures and determine an appropriate baseline workflow. This work showed that a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) was better suited than the initially explored LSTM approach for the available tabular steady-airfoil dataset, and it produced high predictive accuracy for aerodynamic coefficients including $C_L$, $C_D$, $C_M$, and $C_{DP}$. In the Winter 2026 semester, the team extended this work by consolidating the MLP results, improving the interpretation of model behavior, and beginning the transition toward a more ATP-relevant dynamic modeling framework through replication of a published PR-SSM-based method and a published sequence-to-sequence method for predicting three-dimensional unsteady aerodynamic forces and moments. Together, these two semesters of work moved the neural-network effort from initial feasibility demonstration toward a modeling workflow that is better aligned with the final flapping-wing prediction problem.
\par

\underline{Recommended Future Work}

Although the paper replication neural networks performed well, some improvements can be made. For the PR-SSM replication paper, it is suggested that the hyperparameter optimization loop be run with fixed datasets and fixed seeds for more iterations to achieve a higher accuracy. Once the hyperparameter have been optimized for a few fixed initial conditions, randomize the data split and the seed initialization to improve the robustness of the model. For the sequence-to-sequence paper, the model training can be run with a larger batch size and larger temporal windows to improve test accuracy and help the predicted values of the model be closer to the true values. Once these modifications have been implemented, the paper replication models should be transitioned from using the experimental data used in the papers to use experimental data generated by the ATP and RRTS to make three-dimensional force predictions, which will involve modifications to how the data is read, considering the datasets of the papers and test stands are stored in different formats. Additionally, the scope of the NACA airfoil dataset may be increased to include other designations of airfoils, such as all the available airfoils on Airfoil Tools in an attempt to further increase the model accuracy and provide more wing geometries and conditions or to extrapolate from the dataset, to help with potential future proof-of-concept problems in the ATP, RRTS, or other areas of the project that relate to neural networks.
\par}








Finally, the ATPW wings team made steady progress this semester by preparing new wing designs, design of experiment database and establishing the dynamic testing setup needed to evaluate how the wings deform during flapping. The tracking system produced successful preliminary results, giving the team a reliable method for comparing future wing prototypes.

In the Winter 2026 semester, the ATPW team will be starting off strong by focusing on three main objectives. The first will be to fully commission the aerodynamic test platform with a representative MFWF model. This will involve mounting a test flyer to the platform and performing a series of known dynamic loadings to verify the platform's ability to accurately measure forces during flapping.
The wings team will fabricate and test the new wing designs and continue building the dynamic performance database that will support the selection of the final functional wing pair. The neural network team will focus on adapting the MLP model to predict the three-dimensional aerodynamic forces acting on the flapping wings of the MFWF. This will require collaboration with the rest of the ATPW team to collect sufficient data from the test platform to train and validate the model effectively.
