\chapter{Current Advances} 
\sffamily
\label{chap:Literature_Review}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

This report is an overview of the progress that has been made by the aerodynamic test platform and wings team during the Fall 2025 semester. As stated in the summary of this report, the breakdown of this teams structure does not follow one main result, rather it encompasses three. 

\indent For the structural aerodynamic test platform, Stan and Kevin have taken this semester to properly construct and commission it. The prototype that was fabricated from the previous capstone year had an inner frame that was unsuitable for testing, but a solid outer frame. Stan focused his efforts at the start of the semester to design and manufacture a more stable inner frame for the MFWF to securely attach to. During this time, Kevin was using the hardware that was suggested by the previous year to test the electronics for the data acquisition. Unfortunately, after running preliminary loading of the new frame while recording force data, it was discovered that the tension needed to connect the inner frame to the load cells was causing issues resolving the small forces that would be produced by the MFWF. As a result, Stan started investigating how a new design could function without needed any tension on the load cells, and Kevin went through iterations of the electronics to attempt to achieve the high resolution needed to capture the incredibly small forces the flyer will produce. For Stan and Kevin's in-depth advances, see sections \ref{chap:Stan_Melchers} and \ref{chap:Kevin_Fair}, respectfully.

\indent For the ATP neural network, Wade and Aidan have taken on the newly assigned responsibilities to learn about and apply machine learning to the Aerodynamic Test Platform (ATP) of the MFWF. For the Fall 2025 semester, the focus was on developing an understanding of the fundamental principles, methods, and applications of machine learning and applying this knowledge, utilizing NACA airfoil data to synthesize and validate two separate aerodynamic models as a preliminary exercise. For Wade and Aidan's in-depth advances, see sections \ref{chap:Wade_Schnurr} and \ref{chap:Aidan_Sheridan}, respectively.

\indent Abdul and Justin took significant steps in designing, manufacturing, and dynamically screening a structured set of wings for the MFWF flyer. Abdul led the wing design, literature review, manufacturing process development, DOE execution, and design verification. A comprehensive review of published 
literature was conducted to define the deformation 
targets that govern lift generation in hovering flapping wing flight. A revised manufacturing process was developed and validated. The Wings and process was confirmed structurally viable. A provisional wing 
recommendation of R-V-C5.0 (ID:~F) was made based on mass compliance and qualitative camber formation observed during spin testing, pending quantitative ATP validation.

Justin focused on developing a dynamic testing and motion tracking framework to evaluate wing deformation during flapping. This work progressed from initial single-camera manual testing toward a dual-camera configuration. A marker-based tracking system was developed in MATLAB using HSV colour segmentation, along with region filtering and temporal tracking, to help extract displacement data from testing.
 A structured testing environment was established, with ROI masking and span-axis filtering implemented to reduce false detections and improve marker identification. A live HSV sampling tool was also developed to calibrate marker detection under varying lighting conditions. In addition, a checkerboard background was introduced to provide a spatial reference for depth calibration and improve consistency between camera views. 
 For in-depth advances by Abdul and Justin, see sections \ref{chap:Abdulrahman_Sirajudeen-Sanni} and \ref{chap:Justin_Denneny}, respectfully.
